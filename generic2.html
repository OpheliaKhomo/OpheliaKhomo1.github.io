SQL - Student's Mental Health Analysis

Project Overview

In this project, I conducted an analysis to determine how attending a university in a different country can impact a student's mental health. The analysis was based on a dataset published by a Japanese international university, which provided insights into various mental health factors experienced by international students.

Key Findings

  • Students who stayed in the foreign country for a longer period had a high depression rate.
  • Students who stayed in the foreign country for shorter periods had a high acculturative stress.
  • There was a variation/fluctuations in the social connectedness rates, which indicates that the length of stay did not have an impact on how socially connected the students were.
  • Correlation coefficients have indicate a weak or no relationship between the length of stay and mental health changes. This indicates that the factors are not correlated, however the length of stay may be a causation factor.
